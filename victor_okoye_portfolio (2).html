01 / Retail Analytics

Adidas Sales Performance Intelligence Dashboard

Excel Tableau Retail BI Dashboard
The Problem

Retail leadership needed a single source of truth on sales performance — one that could reveal which product categories, regional markets, and sales channels were actually driving revenue, and which were quietly eroding margin without anyone noticing.

The Approach

Cleaned and structured the raw sales dataset in Excel, standardising date formats, resolving category inconsistencies, and computing margin metrics. Built an interactive multi-view dashboard in Tableau segmenting performance across product lines, U.S. regions, retailers, and sales methods. Applied calculated fields, dynamic filters, and conditional formatting to make the dashboard genuinely self-serve for non-technical decision-makers.

Key Findings
  • West region and Men's Street Footwear consistently dominated revenue — revealing a clear geographic and category concentration that the business could double down on.
  • In-store sales outperformed online and outlet channels on revenue per unit, challenging assumptions about digital channel efficiency.
  • Seasonal peaks in Q2 and Q4 were underexploited — inventory and promotional planning was misaligned with demand cycles, costing margin during peak periods.

02 / E-Commerce Analytics

Embrody E-Commerce: Conversion Intelligence Report

Excel Tableau Story Funnel Analysis GIT Challenge
The Problem

Embrody, a global fashion e-commerce brand, was attracting traffic across 7 countries and 5 marketing channels — but haemorrhaging revenue between the browse and the buy. With only a 16.8% overall conversion rate, the business needed to understand where customers were dropping off, which channels were bringing real buyers, and whether their discount strategy was actually working.

The Approach

Cleaned 1,530 customer session records in Excel — resolving missing revenue values, inconsistent date formats, and unreliable cart fields through documented flag columns. Built a 7-chapter interactive Tableau Story answering targeted business questions: channel quality, product revenue drivers, funnel drop-off, market investment priority, discount impact, device performance, and customer satisfaction ratings.

Key Findings
  • Cart abandonment was the single largest revenue leak — 38.3% of customers who added items never completed purchase, representing 365 lost sales per cycle recoverable through cart reminders.
  • Discounts were actively hurting conversions: customers with no discount converted at 41.66% vs. 38.70% for those with a 10% discount — signalling that blanket discounting attracts browsers, not buyers.
  • UAE and Nigeria were the highest-revenue markets; Referral and Email outperformed Instagram on conversion by 6.7 percentage points despite lower traffic volume — a clear case for quality over quantity.

03 / Macroeconomic Research

Nigeria Oil, Naira & Inflation: A Five-Year Economic Autopsy

Excel Tableau Macroeconomics Policy Analysis
The Problem

Between 2020 and 2025, Nigeria experienced one of its most turbulent economic periods — oil revenue shocks from COVID-19, the naira's accelerating devaluation, and inflation climbing to multi-decade highs. Investors, policymakers, and business operators needed a data-grounded narrative of how these forces interacted and where the system broke down.

The Approach

Aggregated five-year data from NNPC, CBN, and World Bank sources across three macroeconomic variables: Brent crude pricing, Nigeria's oil production volumes, and naira exchange rates. Applied the McCandless data storytelling framework to sequence findings into a coherent narrative. Built a multi-panel Tableau dashboard linking oil revenue trends to inflation and FX movements, with annotated period markers for key policy events (subsidy removal, currency floatation).

Key Findings
  • Nigeria's oil production declined even as global Brent prices recovered post-COVID — exposing an infrastructure and security problem that fiscal policy alone cannot fix.
  • Naira devaluation accelerated sharply after the 2023 subsidy removal, with inflation peaking at levels not seen since the 1990s, disproportionately affecting import-dependent sectors.
  • The correlation between oil revenue and FX reserves revealed a structural vulnerability: Nigeria's economic stability remains hostage to a single commodity, with no meaningful diversification buffer built over the five-year period.

04 / Systems & Operations

Optisource IMS: From Paper Ledgers to Digital Intelligence

Python / Flask SQLite Inventory Analytics Supply Chain
The Problem

McDaves, a B2B optical distribution business in Lagos, was managing inventory on paper ledgers and spreadsheets — a system that created chronic stockout blind spots, inaccurate stock counts, and no visibility into sales velocity or supplier performance. The business needed a digital foundation before any analytics work could begin.

The Approach

Designed and built Optisource — a full-stack inventory management system using Python (Flask) and SQLite. The system handles product cataloguing, real-time stock tracking, sales recording, low-stock threshold alerts, and supplier management. Migrated historical paper records into the database schema, and built a simple dashboard layer surfacing key operational KPIs: stock turnover, reorder frequency, and sales trends by product category.

Key Findings
  • Digitalisation revealed that 30%+ of SKUs were chronically overstocked while high-velocity optical frames were consistently running out — a mismatch invisible on paper ledgers.
  • Sales tracking surfaced the top 10 revenue-generating products for the first time, enabling data-driven purchasing decisions that reduced dead stock by an estimated 20%.
  • The system eliminated manual stock reconciliation — reducing end-of-month count time from 2 days to under 3 hours and removing the primary source of inventory data errors.
